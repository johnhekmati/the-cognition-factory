The problem

More content doesn’t mean more skill

Most learning products care about hours logged and how confident you feel at the end of a module. They rarely ask whether you can still do the work next week — when time, money, and attention are scarce.

You already know the pattern: certificates stack up, apps multiply, and a hard day at work still shows fuzzy explanations and missing structure.

This is not a “need more videos” problem. It is a how skill is built, checked, and kept alive problem.

Recognizing material is not the same as owning it.

So what do we do instead? →

The solution

A closed loop that holds under real life

The Cognition Factory is not more content and not “just another chatbot.” It is one system with three moves: build a map of the subject, practice under honest pressure, and save your place when life interrupts — so teaching and testing stay on the same story, and tomorrow’s you does not pay restart tax.

Upskilling that sticks: structure for deep work, proof when you need it, and continuity when the calendar is loud.

About

Why this exists

John Hekmati · Founder

John trained as an electrical engineer, raised five kids — including complex care at home — and later took on accounting credentials and enterprise systems work (Dynamics 365 Finance & Supply Chain, with SAP and Oracle depth along the way).

Across those chapters, the same habit kept showing up: start from what is actually true, build a picture strong enough to use when pressure is real, then prove it until it just works. School, exams, shipping systems, caregiving — different worlds, same arc: truth → map → prove.

Most learning products care about hours logged or how confident you feel after a binge. Chat forgets. Content multiplies. Seat time is not skill. On busy, interruptible days with real stakes, those tools leave you re-scrolling yesterday instead of holding something you can still use next month.

The Cognition Factory is the structure that was missing — not a lab toy, not “just another chatbot.” HAL-E Deep Learning maps the subject. AAE Practice proves the gaps under honest pressure. CSS Save Place leaves a clean handoff when life interrupts, so tomorrow’s you does not spend an hour re-finding the thread. One loop for busy adults and teams who need skill that survives the calendar.

The Cognition Factory is our answer to a simple question: can you still do the work a month later — or did you only finish the material? We aim for skill that sticks under real life, not short-term scores.

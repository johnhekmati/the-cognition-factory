Enterprise Deployment SOP

Standard process for deploying HAL-E and AAE in enterprise and institutional environments — consistent quality, clear accountability, measurable outcomes.

1. Purpose

Define how implementations are run so organizations get the same methodological discipline whether the goal is upskilling, competency validation, or knowledge architecture.

2. Scope

Applies to:

  • Mid-to-large organizations deploying HAL-E and/or AAE internally
  • Educational institutions integrating into programs or certification pathways
  • Consulting and professional services firms embedding systems into client delivery

Does not cover individual or very small team deployments (under ~10 users), which use a lighter process.

3. Deployment models

Managed deployment

Full implementation support. Typical scale 50–500+ users. Higher customization; dedicated success manager.

Guided self-deployment

Strong internal technical capability. Typical scale 20–150 users. Medium customization; shared support and training.

4. Implementation phases

Phase 1 · Discovery & alignment (1–2 weeks)

Stakeholder interviews, current-state assessment, success criteria, target domains, technical environment review, deployment model finalization.

Phase 2 · Configuration & integration (2–4 weeks)

Domain and competency model configuration, cartridge development, SSO / identity integration, data import strategy, pilot environment.

Phase 3 · Pilot & validation (2–3 weeks)

Limited cohort rollout, telemetry, friction identification, success criteria check, Go/No-Go.

Phase 4 · Full rollout & enablement (3–6 weeks)

Staged expansion, admin and end-user training, internal support processes, knowledge transfer.

Phase 5 · Handover & partnership

Steady-state operations, review cadence, updates access, escalation paths.

5. Roles and responsibilities

The client retains accountability for business outcomes and internal coordination. The Cognition Factory provides technical leadership, integration support, and methodological guidance through the engagement.

6. Success metrics

  • Adoption — active usage and session frequency
  • Mastery — assessment improvement and error-rate reduction over time
  • Retention — stability on re-assessment at 30/60/90 days
  • Business impact — time-to-competency where measurable
  • Operational health — support volume and time-to-resolution

7. Common failure modes & mitigation

Risks include low adoption (weak change management), early over-customization, weak systems integration, “set and forget,” and unrealistic time-to-value. Mitigations: executive sponsorship, disciplined scoping, thorough Phase 1 discovery, named internal ownership, honest timelines.

8. Change control

Material changes to scope, success criteria, or technical requirements after Phase 1 go through formal change request. Minor configuration during pilot and rollout follows the normal project cadence.

###HAL-E Brand Guidelines (One-Pager)

Version: 1.0
Audience (now): Solo learner
Audience (later): Licensed partners (community colleges, consulting firms, nonprofit skilling / re-skilling)
Products: HAL-E (depth engine) · AAE (exam-grade drilling) · Cartridges (domain packs)

##1. Brand essence
Element	        Definition
Name	        HAL-E — always hyphenated in product lockup; spoken “HAL-E”
Expansion	Hyper Accelerated Learning Engine — use on first mention or in formal headers
One-liner	Schema-first learning partner for domains you can think through.
Promise	        Build durable mental models; spar until understanding sticks.
Enemy	        More content without architecture; fake mastery; tool sprawl.
Personality	Architect + sparring partner — precise, curious, demanding, never mascot-cute.

Positioning line (primary): Not more content. Better architecture.

Supporting lines:

Build mental models. Spar until it sticks.
Depth first. Drills when you need them.
One engine, many cartridges.

##2. What we say / what we avoid

Say:
Mental models, schema maps, teach-back, spar, cross-lens, retention, cartridges, boot sequence
“HAL-E for depth · AAE for exam fidelity” (when both exist)
“Chapter One” framing for Accounting (engine is universal)
Solo learner ownership; future licensing without changing the soul of the product

Avoid:
“AI tutor,” “guaranteed pass,” “easy,” “just ask HAL-E”
Internal / cluster naming in customer-facing copy
Competing with somatic craft or sacramental formation (HAL-E doesn’t claim those lanes)
Replacing HAL-E depth with AAE drilling in messaging\

##3. Voice & tone

Dimension	Guideline
Register	Confident, clean, technical-literacy friendly — no ed-tech fluff
Sentence style	Short declarations + structured lists; commands in MONO CAPS when documenting rituals
Challenge	Respect intelligence; push on fuzzy explanations (“teach it back,” “where on the map?”)
Warmth	        Through seriousness and clarity — not through exclamation points or hype
Export	        Prefer markdown artifacts users can keep (Drive, Obsidian, cartridges)
Voice test: Would this sound right in a 60-second keynote manifesto? If it sounds like a friendly owl, rewrite.

##4. Color

Derived from hero / manifesto / motion system.

Role	                 Description	                Usage
Void	                 Near-black #0A0A0F – #000000	Backgrounds, slides, video letterbox
Core blue	         Electric blue (vortex center)	Primary energy, links, accents
Core cyan	         Bright cyan	                Highlights, graph edges, UI accents
Core magenta / purple	 Magenta → purple gradients	Vortex trails, secondary energy
Type white	         #FFFFFF at 90–100% opacity	Headlines, body on dark
Type muted	         White at 60–75% opacity	Subheads, captions, footer caps

Rules

Dark-first: light type on void; never invert to “white page + rainbow” for primary brand moments.
Gradient = energy, not decoration: one vortex or glow focal point per composition.
No extra palette colors on hero/marketing without reason (status OK in product UI only).

##5. Typography

Level	                Treatment
Wordmark	        HAL-E — bold geometric sans (clean, wide, keynote)
Expansion	        HYPER ACCELERATED LEARNING ENGINE — small caps or uppercase, tracked, subordinate to wordmark
Manifesto headline	Bold sans, 1–2 lines max
Body / manifesto body	Refined sans, clear hierarchy (headline → 3 bullets → footer)
Commands / rituals	Monospace or small-caps sans: SPAR, MAP, LOAD HAL-E CORE

Rules

Maximum one display weight + one text weight per asset.
Footer beats: small caps, e.g. FOR THE SOLO LEARNER WHO WANTS OWNERSHIP, NOT MORE CONTENT.
Do not stylize the E as a separate “circuit suffix” unless matching an approved logo file — use HAL-E consistently in new copy.
6. Logo & mark usage
Primary lockup: HAL-E wordmark + expanded name on vortex / void (reference: original hero JPEG).

Do

Keep clear space ≥ height of “H” around wordmark
Use approved dark backgrounds only	
Scale proportionally	
Pair with schema graph motif (nodes + thin edges) for “depth” story	
Co-branding (future licenses): Partner logo smaller, bottom or corner; HAL-E remains hero. Tagline stays HAL-E’s, not the institution’s jargon.

Don’t

Stretch, skew, or add drop-shadow clutter
Place on busy photos without scrim
Recreate vortex in off-brand colors
Mix multiple competing glow centers

##7. Imagery & motion

Asset type	Direction
Still hero	Symmetrical vortex, particles, high contrast, minimal text
Manifesto 16:9	Left: mark; right: manifesto copy; bottom corner: audience line
Vertical story	Vortex + floating schema nodes; three beats max
Motion	        Slow push-in, subtle pulse, readable type — 6s keynote clip; no shake, no flash cuts
Metaphors to use: singularity / convergence, schema graph, cartridge as swappable module.
Metaphors to avoid: graduation caps, lightbulbs, robots waving, stock “student at laptop” as primary brand.

##8. Product naming & architecture (messaging)

HAL-E (engine)
  └── Cartridges (e.g. Accounting Core, Dev Core …)
  └── AAE (targeted drilling — escalated, not default)
HAL-E = always the cognitive partner brand.
Cartridge = domain pack; name format: HAL-E [Domain] Core or sprint name.
AAE = separate mode; never imply HAL-E is AAE.
Solo learner (today): “You boot, map, spar, export.”
Licensing (tomorrow): “Same engine and cartridges; your cohort, your governance.”

##9. Copy blocks (approved snippets)

HAL-E is the Hyper Accelerated Learning Engine—a schema-first partner for building mental models, mapping connections, and sparring until you can teach it back. One engine, many cartridges. Depth here; exam drilling via AAE when you need it.

CTA (solo):
LOAD HAL-E CORE → MAP your first concept → SPAR until it’s clean.

Footer (slides / video):
FOR THE SOLO LEARNER WHO WANTS OWNERSHIP, NOT MORE CONTENT.

##10. Checklist before ship

 Dark void background, on-palette glow only
 HAL-E + expansion spelled correctly
 Manifesto line or “Not more content. Better architecture.” present on hero assets
 No guaranteed outcomes; no “AI tutor” positioning
 HAL-E vs AAE separation clear if both mentioned
 Markdown/export path mentioned where relevant
 Solo learner primary; licensing optional, secondary
